Cyber Hygiene

Author: Soumya Agarwal

Incidents of cybercrime have surged by 500% largely due to limited awareness regarding cyber hygiene. It is important to note that while the internet is useful and convenient, it is not inherently safe. Cybercriminals monitor your every move online; a single slip-up can land you in the clutches of fraudsters. Just as you maintain cleanliness in your surroundings to ward off diseases, you must remain vigilant against the rising cyber threats that make people victims of fraud. Incorporating cyber hygiene practices into your daily routine is essential to minimize cyber risks.

While for us, cyber security may be a matter of occasional vigilance and awareness, for cyber fraudsters, it is a full-time job. Recent times have seen a rise in scams involving fake vehicle auctions, share trading fraud, and UPI scams. "Digital arrests" have now become a routine tactic for cybercriminals. Furthermore, the advent of AI tools has effectively handed cyber fraudsters a "magic lamp" (an incredibly powerful resource).

In reality, before executing a scam, cybercriminals employ various psychological tactics to gain people's trust and lure them into a trap. To avoid falling victim to their schemes, we must keep a few simple precautions in mind.

Data Backup

Despite numerous security measures, the threat of ransomware and malware continues to grow. Hackers often lock computers or threaten to delete data if their demands are not met. Phishing emails and fake online advertisements are frequently used to facilitate such cyberattacks.

Use of Antivirus

Using a reliable antivirus tool for your device is crucial. Antivirus software mitigates threats associated with ransomware, spyware, and similar risks, thereby reducing the likelihood of your data falling into the wrong hands. Additionally, avoid reusing old passwords. You can use multi-factor authentication to secure your account; this provides an extra layer of protection.

Keep software up-to-date

It is essential to regularly update your operating system, browsers, and apps. Outdated software and devices are easy targets for cybercriminals, as manufacturers often stop releasing updates for older models, thereby increasing cybersecurity risks. Enable automatic software updates so you can install them as soon as notifications are received.

Exercise caution when sharing information

If a significant amount of your personal data is already available on social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and X, you could become an easy target for cybercriminals. Such information makes it easier for them to guess your passwords or target you through phishing attacks; scammers can use these details to craft fake messages aimed at you. You should continuously monitor your social media accounts, bank accounts, and credit cards for any unusual activity.

Watch out for suspicious activity

If you detect any tampering with your financial or personal data, or notice anything unusual, report it to the relevant authorities immediately. Large-scale fraud involving KYC scams is currently prevalent; if someone calls claiming to be a bank official or a parcel delivery agent, verify the claim through official websites or apps first. Awareness is key. Implementing multi-factor authentication is a wise move for account security, as it offers additional protection—such as receiving a code on your phone or verifying via Google Authenticator—in addition to your password.
